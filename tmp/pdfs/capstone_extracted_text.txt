INTRODUCTION
Project Context
In the Philippines, event-based services such as magic shows, puppet shows, bubble shows, and
party hosting are often managed through manual methods, including phone calls, social media
messages, handwritten schedules, and personal messaging applications. While these practices are
commonly used, they often result in operational issues such as double booking, schedule conflicts,
delayed confirmations, missed appointments, inaccurate event details, and poor record
management. These problems become more significant as the number of clients and bookings
increases, particularly during peak seasons such as holidays, school activities, fiestas, and
corporate events.
These challenges are evident in the case of Aries the Magic Artist, a professional event entertainer
who offers services such as hosting, magic performances, illusions, bubble shows, and puppet
shows for various occasions. Based on the face-to-face interview conducted by the proponents, it
was found that the business still relies on a manual booking and scheduling process. As a result,
managing appointment details, confirming bookings, and organizing client records becomes
difficult and time-consuming. This manual approach increases the risk of scheduling conflicts,
delayed responses, and inefficient handling of booking information, which may affect both
business operations and client satisfaction.
For independent performers and small-scale event service providers, the absence of a centralized
and computerized system makes it difficult to efficiently manage client information, service
packages, schedules, and booking records. Manual processes are more vulnerable to human error
and often lack the accuracy, accessibility, and organization needed for daily operations. In service-
oriented businesses, these limitations may reduce work efficiency and negatively affect the quality
and reliability of service provided to clients.

Purpose and Description
The purpose of this project is to design and develop an organized online booking and scheduling
management system for professional event entertainers that automates key processes such as client
booking, service selection, scheduling, payment handling, and event management. The system
serves as a centralized digital platform where clients can submit booking requests, select event
details, choose service packages, upload proof of downpayment, request rescheduling, and monitor
the status of their transactions.
The system also provides separate role-based modules for the Owner, Admin, and Staff. The
Owner is responsible for final business-related decisions such as payment verification, refund
verification, calendar control, reschedule approval, and reports. The admin manages system
operations such as booking records, service packages, user accounts, calendar settings, and system
monitoring. The Staff supports daily operations by monitoring bookings, schedules, reschedule
requests, completed events, and service-related tasks.
This purpose is aligned with the first objective of the study, which focuses on designing an online
booking module that allows clients to select services, event details, date, time, location, and
packages through a digital platform. To support this objective, the system provides a structured
booking process that includes account registration, secure login, booking request submission,
package selection, payment submission, booking status monitoring, notifications, and reschedule
request functionality. By integrating these features into one centralized platform, the system

Scope and Limitations
Scope
The proposed Online Booking and Scheduling Management System for Professional Event
Entertainers Service covers the major operations needed to manage client bookings, service
packages, schedules, payments, refund requests, reschedule requests, reports, notifications, and
system records. The system is divided into four main user roles: Client, Owner, Admin, and Staff.
Each role has specific access and responsibilities based on its function in the booking and
scheduling process.
The role classification of the system is designed to maintain an organized workflow and proper
access control. The Client submits and monitors booking-related transactions. The Owner handles
final business decisions such as payment verification, refund verification, calendar control,
reschedule approval, and reports. The admin manages system operations, booking records, service
packages, user accounts, and calendar settings. The Staff supports daily monitoring of booking
records, schedules, reschedule requests, completed events, and service-related tasks.
Client Module
The Client Module is designed for customers who want to view, book, and monitor
entertainment services online. This module allows clients to access service information, submit
booking requests, upload proof of payment, track booking status, request refunds when
applicable, request rescheduling, and receive system notifications. It provides clients with a
convenient and organized platform for completing booking-related transactions without relying
only on manual messages, calls, or social media communication.
